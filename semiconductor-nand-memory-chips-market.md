<p><p>The semiconductor NAND memory chips market consists of three main types: SLC (Single-Level Cell), MLC (Multi-Level Cell), and TLC (Triple-Level Cell). SLC stores one bit per cell, offering superior performance and endurance, making it ideal for high-end applications. MLC stores two bits per cell, providing a balance between cost and performance for consumer electronics. TLC stores three bits per cell, maximizing storage density and reducing costs but at the expense of speed and longevity. Each type serves distinct market needs.</p></p>
