<p><strong>The Reference Electrode Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Aqueous Reference Electrode</li><li>Nonaqueous Reference Electrode</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Reference electrodes are used in electrochemical measurements to provide a stable reference potential for comparison with another electrode. Aqueous reference electrodes are specifically designed for use in aqueous solutions, while nonaqueous reference electrodes are used in nonaqueous solutions. Aqueous electrodes typically utilize a redox couple (e.g. Ag/AgCl) in a salt bridge, while nonaqueous electrodes may use materials such as lithium or sodium. Both types of electrodes play crucial roles in a variety of industries including research, environmental monitoring, and industrial processes.</p></p>
