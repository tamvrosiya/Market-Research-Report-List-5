<p><p>Implantable Ventricular Assist Devices (VADs) are devices used to help the heart pump blood to the rest of the body. There are three main types of VADs: Left Ventricular Assist Devices (LVADs) which help the left side of the heart, Right Ventricular Assist Devices (RVADs) which help the right side of the heart, and BiVADs which assist both sides of the heart. These devices are used in patients with advanced heart failure or those awaiting a heart transplant to improve heart function and quality of life.</p></p>

<p><p>Implantable Ventricular Assist Devices (VAD) are used in three main applications in the market. The Bridge-to-transplant (BTT) application is for patients awaiting heart transplantation, providing temporary support until a donor heart becomes available. Destination Therapy (DT) involves long-term support for patients who are not eligible for transplantation. Other applications include Bridge-to-recovery and Bridge-to-candidacy, where VADs are used to stabilize and improve the patient's condition before further treatment. These applications showcase the diverse uses of VADs in providing life-saving support for patients with heart failure.</p></p>
