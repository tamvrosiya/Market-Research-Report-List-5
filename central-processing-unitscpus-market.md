<p><p>The Central Processing Units (CPUs) market includes two main types: Ball Grid Array (BGA) and Chip Scale Package (CSP). BGAs are designed with solder balls on the underside of the chip for mounting onto a circuit board, providing high performance in a compact size. On the other hand, CSPs are even smaller and have an integrated circuit package that is the same size as the chip itself, allowing for even greater space efficiency. Both BGA and CSP CPUs cater to different market needs and applications.</p></p>
