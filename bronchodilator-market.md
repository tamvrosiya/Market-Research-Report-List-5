<p><p>The bronchodilator market is categorized into short-acting and long-acting bronchodilators. Short-acting bronchodilators provide rapid relief from asthma and COPD symptoms, typically lasting for 4 to 6 hours, and are often used as rescue medications. In contrast, long-acting bronchodilators offer prolonged symptom control, lasting over 12 hours, and are used for regular management of chronic respiratory conditions. Both types are essential for improving airflow, reducing breathlessness, and enhancing patients' overall quality of life in managing respiratory diseases.</p></p>
