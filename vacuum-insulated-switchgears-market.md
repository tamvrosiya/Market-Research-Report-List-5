<p><p>Primary switchgear is the main control point for electrical power within a facility, typically receiving power directly from the utility grid. It is responsible for protecting and controlling the flow of electricity to various circuits and equipment within a facility. On the other hand, secondary switchgear is used to distribute power throughout a facility and includes devices such as circuit breakers and relays. Both types of switchgear play crucial roles in ensuring the safe and efficient distribution of electricity within a facility.</p></p>
