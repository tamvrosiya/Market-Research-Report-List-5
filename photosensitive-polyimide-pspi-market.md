<p><p>Photosensitive Polyimide (PSPI) is a specialized polymer used in the production of high-performance films and coatings, particularly in microelectronics and semiconductor applications. The market is categorized into two types: Positive Photosensitive Polyimide, which becomes soluble upon exposure to light, making it suitable for applications requiring high-resolution patterns, and Negative Photosensitive Polyimide, which becomes insoluble upon exposure, providing enhanced durability and thermal stability. Both types are essential in advanced manufacturing processes, enabling precise microfabrication.</p></p>
