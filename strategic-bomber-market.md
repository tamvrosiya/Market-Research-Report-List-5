<p><p>Strategic bomber market types are differentiated based on their propulsion systems. Turbofan bombers utilize a combination of thrust from a gas turbine engine and a fan to generate power. Turbojet bombers rely solely on exhaust gases expelled from a gas turbine for propulsion. Turboprop bombers use a gas turbine engine to drive a propeller, which produces thrust. Each type offers advantages in terms of speed, fuel efficiency, and range, allowing military forces to select the most suitable platform for their strategic bombing missions.</p></p>
