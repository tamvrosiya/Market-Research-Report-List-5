<p><p>The Automatic Voltage Regulator (AVR) market consists of three main types: Linear Regulators, Shunt Voltage Regulators, and Others. Linear regulators provide a constant output voltage by dissipating excess power as heat, making them efficient but less energy-saving. Shunt voltage regulators regulate voltage by shunting current away from the load, making them ideal for high-power applications. The "Others" category includes various types of voltage regulators such as switching regulators and buck-boost regulators, offering a wider range of features and capabilities.</p></p>
