<p><p>Polymer gas separation membranes are advanced materials utilized for the selective separation of gases from mixtures based on differences in permeability. These membranes offer advantages such as energy efficiency, lower operational costs, and compact size compared to traditional gas separation technologies like adsorption and cryogenic distillation. This evolution positions polymer gas separation membranes as a pivotal solution in addressing global energy and environmental challenges.</p></p>

<p><p>The polymer gas separation membrane market is segmented into three main types: hollow fiber, spiral wound, and others. Hollow fiber membranes consist of numerous thin fibers, providing a large surface area and efficient gas separation. Spiral wound membranes are constructed by wrapping two or more layers of membrane material around a central tube, enhancing compactness and performance. Other types may include flat sheet and capillary membranes, each offering distinct advantages based on application requirements and operational conditions.</p></p>
