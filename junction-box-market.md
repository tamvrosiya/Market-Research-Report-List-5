<p><p>Junction boxes are essential components used in electrical installations to protect wire connections and facilitate maintenance. Non-metallic junction boxes are typically made of plastic or fiberglass, offering high durability and resistance to corrosion. Metallic junction boxes, on the other hand, are constructed from materials like steel or aluminum, providing robust protection against physical damage and electromagnetic interference. Both types of junction boxes come in various shapes and sizes to accommodate different installation needs in residential, commercial, and industrial settings.</p></p>
