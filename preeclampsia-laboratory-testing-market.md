<p><p>Preeclampsia laboratory testing involves a combination of blood tests and urine tests to monitor and diagnose the condition in pregnant women. Blood tests are used to measure levels of proteins, liver enzymes, and platelets that can indicate the presence of preeclampsia. Urine tests, on the other hand, are done to check for proteinuria, which is a common symptom of the condition. These tests are crucial in the early detection and management of preeclampsia to ensure the health and safety of both the mother and the baby.</p></p>
