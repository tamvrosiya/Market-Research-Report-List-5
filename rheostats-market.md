<p><ul><li>Digital Rheostats</li><li>Mechanical Rheostats</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Rheostats are variable resistors used to control the flow of electric current in a circuit. Digital rheostats utilize electronic components to adjust resistance digitally, offering precise control and programmable settings. Mechanical rheostats use physical mechanisms, such as rotating dials or sliders, to adjust resistance manually. Both types are widely used in various industries, including electronics, telecommunications, and automotive. While digital rheostats provide advanced features and automation capabilities, mechanical rheostats are simpler and more cost-effective for basic applications.</p></p>
