<p><p>Light Rail Vehicles (LRVs) can be categorized into two main market types based on capacity: Lower Capacity and Higher Capacity. Lower Capacity LRVs typically have a smaller passenger capacity per vehicle and are better suited for intra-city transit systems with shorter distances between stops. On the other hand, Higher Capacity LRVs are designed to accommodate a larger number of passengers and are commonly used in higher demand urban areas or for longer commuter routes. Both types of LRVs play a critical role in providing efficient and sustainable public transportation options.</p></p>
