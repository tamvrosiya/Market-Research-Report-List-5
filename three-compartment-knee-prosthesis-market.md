<p><p>Cemented bone knee prostheses involve using a special bone cement to secure the implant into place, providing immediate stability and support. On the other hand, non-cemented bone knee prostheses rely on the bone growing into the implant over time for stability. Both types have their advantages and disadvantages, with cemented prostheses offering quicker recovery and non-cemented prostheses potentially lasting longer. Patients and surgeons can choose the type of knee prosthesis based on factors such as age, activity level, and bone quality.</p></p>
