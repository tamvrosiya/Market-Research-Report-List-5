<p><p>The Dry Cream Substitute market includes two main types: Powder and Liquid. Powder dry cream substitutes are typically made from a mix of vegetable fats, powdered milk, and stabilizers for texture. They are popular for their convenience and long shelf life. Liquid dry cream substitutes are usually a mixture of water, vegetable oils, emulsifiers, and flavorings. They are convenient for quick use in cooking and baking. Both types cater to consumers looking for dairy-free or lower-fat alternatives to traditional cream.</p></p>
