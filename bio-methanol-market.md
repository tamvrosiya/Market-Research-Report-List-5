<p><p>The bio methanol market can be categorized into two primary types based on the sourcing of feedstock: product-sourced and waste-sourced. Product-sourced bio methanol involves utilizing renewable resources, such as biomass or specific agricultural products, to produce methanol. In contrast, waste-sourced bio methanol derives from organic waste materials, including municipal solid waste or industrial by-products, which are converted into methanol. Both types provide sustainable alternatives to fossil fuels, contributing to a circular economy and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.</p></p>
