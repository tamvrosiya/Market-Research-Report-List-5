<p><p>Solder preforms are commonly used in surface mount technology (SMT) for creating reliable solder joints in electronic assemblies. There are two main types of solder preforms for the SMT market: leaded and lead-free. Leaded solder preforms contain tin and lead, while lead-free solder preforms are made of alloys such as tin-silver-copper. Both types provide precise amounts of solder for connecting components on circuit boards during the assembly process. The choice between leaded and lead-free solder preforms depends on the specific requirements of the application and compliance with environmental regulations.</p></p>
